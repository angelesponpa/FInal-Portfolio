About Me

I am Angeles Ponpa, a 2024-2025 graduate journalism student at Northwestern University’s Medill School of Journalism, specializing in Politics, Policy, and Foreign Affairs with a focus on data-driven reporting.

My passion for journalism began at a young age when I conducted my first interviews on a local food shortage in my hometown of Aurora, IL. Over time, my interest in politics grew, leading me to earn an undergraduate degree in Political Science and Sociology. I worked on political campaigns, ran for local office, and served in the Illinois Senate, but ultimately, I realized my true calling was in journalism—informing the public with accurate, compelling, and impactful stories.

Today, my work focuses on Mexican politics, U.S.-Mexico relations, immigration, and Catholic affairs worldwide. Through multimedia storytelling and data-driven analysis, I strive to deliver insightful, well-researched journalism that engages and informs audiences.
